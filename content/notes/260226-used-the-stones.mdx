I've been using AI agents to contribute to AI-related open source projects. It feels like the Thanos line from Endgame:

<Callout emoji="🪨">
"I used the stones to destroy the stones." — Thanos, Endgame
</Callout>

Except - there's a theory the stones weren't destroyed. Reduced to atoms. Still existing, dissolved into the substrate of everything.

That's closer to what's actually happening.

There's a long tradition of this in CS. [GCC](https://gcc.gnu.org/) compiles GCC. The [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/) compiler is written in Rust. [PyPy](https://pypy.org/) is Python written in Python. A language bootstrapping itself is historically the sign it's arrived - stable and expressive enough to build more of itself.

AI contributing to AI repos feels like that moment. Not a paradox. A milestone.

The stones didn't disappear. They became atoms. Building material for whatever comes next.
